# Vietnam DOOH Advertiser Spending Analysis
## Who's Paying the Most Money into Digital Out-of-Home Advertising

**Report Date:** December 2, 2025
**Market Focus:** Vietnam DOOH Advertising Buyers & Spending Patterns
**Time Period:** 2024-2025 Market Intelligence

---

## Executive Summary

This report provides detailed intelligence on advertiser spending in Vietnam's Digital Out-of-Home (DOOH) advertising market, identifying top spenders, budget allocations, and buyer behavior patterns.

**Key Findings:**
- **Retail & Consumer Goods** dominate with 44.49% market share
- **FMCG** commands 33.71% of digital advertising spend
- **91 of Vietnam's top 100 companies** actively invest in DOOH
- **Total market size:** USD $129.58M (2025) growing to $171.29M (2030)
- **Geographic concentration:** HCMC and Hanoi capture majority of spending

---

### Seasonal Spending Patterns

#### Tet (Lunar New Year) - Traditional Peak Period

**Historical Patterns:**
- Spirits brands: **60% of yearly sales** in 6 weeks leading to Tet
- Beer sales: **+30%** during Tet period
- Soft drinks, biscuits, candy: significant increases
- Historically highest DOOH spending period

**2024 Tet Disruption - Market Shift:**
- **47% year-over-year decline** in consumer engagement during Tet 2024
- Family reunion discussions: **-77.5%** on social media
- Traditional nostalgia-based Tet campaigns losing effectiveness

**Strategic Implication:**
- Brands must innovate beyond traditional Tet messaging
- Diversify seasonal spend, don't over-concentrate on Tet
- Year-round programmatic optimization increasingly preferred

---

#### Other Seasonal Considerations

**Entertainment:**
- Film release calendar synchronization

- Tactical high-impact bursts

**Automotive:**
- Model year launches (Q4-Q1)

**Retail:**
- Back-to-school

- Year-end shopping season

**FMCG:**

## 5. MARKET DYNAMICS & SPENDING DATA

### Total Advertising Spend Flowing into DOOH

#### 2024 Baseline Figures
- **Digital OOH:** USD $107.50 million (primary source)
- **Alternative estimate:** USD $32.60 million (conservative Statista forecast)
- **Total OOH (Static + Digital):** Approximately USD $500 million (2024)
- **Static OOH share:** 75.24% of revenue (2024)
- **Digital OOH share:** 24.76% of revenue (2024)

#### 2025 Projections
- **Total OOH/DOOH Market:** USD $129.58 million (combined)
- **Vietnam Total Advertising Market:** USD $2.93 billion (2025)
- **DOOH as % of Total Ad Market:** 3.6-11% depending on methodology

---

### Growth Rates & Projections

#### Short-Term (2024-2025)
- **Digital OOH:** 12.54% CAGR through 2030
- **Static OOH:** Slower growth, maintaining 75.24% share
- **Programmatic DOOH:** 7.19% CAGR (Southeast Asia regional)

#### Long-Term (2024-2030)
- **Total market:** 5.74% CAGR (conservative)
- **Digital OOH:** USD $39.1M to **USD $282.40M by 2033** (10.14% CAGR - aggressive projection)
- **Mall/Retail Interior:** 7.70% CAGR - projected USD $15.4M by 2030

---

#### Sector-Specific Growth Rates
- **Telecom/Technology:** 7.04% CAGR (highest)
- **Retail:** 44.49% increase in 2024 spending (one-year surge)
- **Automotive:** Fastest budget increases currently (qualitative)
- **FMCG:** 61.8% platform revenue uplift (JCDecaux H1 2024)

---

### Key Growth Drivers

1. **Urbanization**
   - Rapid metro expansion in HCMC and Hanoi
   - New inventory creation in transit environments

2. **Smart City Infrastructure**
   - Municipal buildings mandating digital signage
   - Government-driven digital transformation

3. **Programmatic Adoption**
   - Platforms: ANTS, VIOOH/JCDecaux, Moving Walls
   - Enabling efficiency and optimization
   - Real-time buying capabilities

4. **5G Rollout**
   - Telecom competition fueling ad spend
   - Enhanced interactive capabilities
   - Mobile-to-DOOH integration

5. **E-commerce Pressure**
   - Retailers fighting back with physical store traffic drivers
   - Omnichannel strategies requiring DOOH component

6. **Measurement Improvement**
   - Data-rich screens enabling attribution
   - Programmatic platforms providing analytics
   - ROI demonstration improving

#### Unique DOOH Advantages Driving Investment

1. **Cannot be blocked** (vs. digital ad blockers)

6. **Large format impact** impossible in other channels

## 6. GEOGRAPHIC SPENDING PREFERENCES

### City Hierarchy & Investment Levels

#### Tier 1A: Ho Chi Minh City (HCMC)

**Market Leadership:**
- Generates "lion's share" of DOOH spending
- Population: 9.4 million (largest ad market)
- HCMC and Hanoi combined: "disproportionately large share" of total revenue

**Infrastructure Advantage:**
- Metro project seeding "hundreds of interior screens"
- Rapid LED concessions creating premium supply
- Fresh inventory attracting advertiser investment

**Advertiser Behavior:**
- Gravitating toward data-rich digital screens
- Premium CPMs acceptable for quality inventory
- High concentration of 91 top-100 companies

---

#### Tier 1B: Hanoi

**Market Position:**
- Second largest market
- Smart-city plan mandating digital signage in municipal buildings

**Digital Consumer Behavior:**
- Residents spend MORE time online: **3.1 hours/day**
- vs. HCMC: 2.6 hours/day
- Slightly more digital format lean

**Advertiser Strategy:**
- Data-rich digital screens seeing strong demand
- Tech/fintech brands over-indexing
- Government building digital signage creating new inventory

---

#### Tier 2: Da Nang & Secondary Cities

**Market Characteristics:**
- Static formats "retain resilience" (less digital penetration)
- Urbanization driving **6.29% CAGR** for overall market
- Lower CPMs enabling volume plays

**Advertiser Strategy:**
- Broader reach campaigns
- Value-tier pricing
- Emerging neighborhoods for growth brands
