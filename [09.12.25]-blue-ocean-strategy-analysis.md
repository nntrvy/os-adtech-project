## 1. INDUSTRY RED OCEAN ANALYSIS

### Current Competitive Factors (Strategy Canvas)

**Traditional DOOH competitors are fighting over:**

1. **Premium static locations** - Airports, malls, highways (limited inventory)
2. **High upfront infrastructure costs** - $200k-500k for LED installations
3. **Long-term venue partnerships** - Negotiating with landlords, municipalities
4. **Static placement** - Billboards don't move, coverage is fixed
5. **Manual campaign updates** - Requires physical installation or manual digital updates
6. **High pricing** - $2,000-10,000/month per billboard location
7. **Limited measurement** - Basic impression estimates, no real attribution
8. **Programmatic complexity** - Expensive SSP/DSP integrations
9. **Urban-only coverage** - Concentrated in HCMC/Hanoi Districts 1
10. **Format limitations** - Video requires expensive LED infrastructure

**Where competitors over-serve:**
- Chicilon Media: 93% elevator market share but captive, stationary audience
- DatVietVAC/Vie BOARD: 25+ years experience but legacy static infrastructure
- JCDecaux: International brand but expensive premium positioning
- Bizman Media: Massive LED installations (SKYLED) but $200k+ capex per project

**Industry sacred cows everyone accepts:**
- Digital DOOH requires expensive custom hardware
- Screens must be in fixed, premium locations
- Video is essential for modern DOOH
- Programmatic requires sophisticated platform from day 1
- Manual sales is the only way to launch

---

### Non-Customer Opportunities

**Tier 1: Soon-to-be non-customers (on the edge)**
- Small/medium local brands priced out by $2k-10k/month billboards
- E-commerce brands wanting physical presence but can't afford traditional DOOH
- Seasonal/event-based advertisers needing short bursts (traditional DOOH has long minimums)

**Tier 2: Refusing non-customers (consciously rejected DOOH)**
- Performance marketers who can't measure DOOH ROI
- Mobile-first brands with no physical retail (can't justify DOOH without attribution)
- Startups/SMEs who see DOOH as "enterprise only"

**Tier 3: Unexplored non-customers (never considered)**
- Hyperlocal businesses (neighborhood restaurants, salons) - taxis can target specific routes
- Real-time advertisers (flash sales, inventory clearance) - need same-day updates
- Gig economy platforms (Grab Food merchants, local delivery) - need affordable reach

## 2. BLUE OCEAN INSPIRATION

### Relevant Blue Ocean Case Studies

#### Case 1: Southwest Airlines - Relevance Score: 9/10

**What they eliminated:**
- Seat assignments
- Meals
- Lounges
- Hub-and-spoke routing

**What they created:**
- Point-to-point routes
- Friendly service
- Fun flying experience
- Low-cost accessibility

**Lesson for Taxi DOOH:**
Eliminate industry assumptions (expensive hardware, video, programmatic from day 1) to create a fundamentally simpler, more accessible product. Make DOOH available to the "non-flyers" of advertising.

#### Case 2: Cirque du Soleil - Relevance Score: 8/10

**What they eliminated:**
- Animal acts
- Star performers
- Multiple show arenas

**What they created:**
- Artistic theater-circus fusion
- Themed intellectual experience
- Refined, adult-focused environment

**Lesson for Taxi DOOH:**
Stop competing on traditional DOOH factors (biggest screens, premium locations, video). Instead, create new value: mobility, real-time updates, hyperlocal targeting, affordability. Attract advertisers who never considered DOOH before.

#### Case 3: Nintendo Wii - Relevance Score: 9/10

- Cutting-edge graphics
- Complex controllers

- Motion controls

Don't compete on specs (screen size, resolution, video). Compete on accessibility (affordable, self-serve, real-time updates). Target the "non-gamers" - small brands, e-commerce, hyperlocal businesses that traditional DOOH ignores.

## 6. GAP ANALYSIS & RECOMMENDATIONS

### Red Ocean Risks (Avoid These Traps)

**Risk 1: Feature Creep Toward Traditional DOOH**
- Trap: Adding video, premium screens, programmatic from day 1
- Why dangerous: Destroys cost advantage, delays validation
- Mitigation: Stick to ruthless MVP scope (static images, manual upload)

**Risk 2: Enterprise Sales Dependency**
- Trap: Only targeting top 100 brands like competitors
- Why dangerous: Long sales cycles, high CAC, competitive bidding
- Mitigation: Build self-serve for SMEs simultaneously (blue ocean segment)

**Risk 3: Premium Pricing Positioning**
- Trap: Pricing at market rate ($2k-5k/month) because "you can"
- Why dangerous: Loses differentiation, competes head-to-head
- Mitigation: Price disruptively low ($500-1k/week) to open new markets

**Risk 4: Static Location Thinking**
- Trap: Treating taxis like billboards (measure by location not mobility)
- Why dangerous: Misses the core innovation advantage
- Mitigation: Market "citywide mobile coverage" not "50k locations"

**Risk 5: Technology Over-Engineering**
- Trap: Building Grab-level ad server with 7-stage pipeline from day 1
- Why dangerous: $500k+ engineering cost, 6-month delay
- Mitigation: Manual uploads → Simple targeting → ML later (phased approach)

---

### Blue Ocean Opportunities (Maximize These)

**Opportunity 1: SME Self-Serve Portal**
- Current: Traditional DOOH requires agency/enterprise sales
- Blue Ocean: Web platform where local restaurant owner uploads ad in 5 min
- Impact: 10x customer acquisition vs enterprise-only model
- Timing: Phase 2 (Month 6-12 after MVP validation)

**Opportunity 2: Performance Pricing Models**
- Current: DOOH is CPM-only (impressions)
- Blue Ocean: Offer CPC (clicks via QR codes) and CPA (purchases)
- Impact: Attracts performance marketers who avoid traditional DOOH
- Timing: Phase 2, requires QR tracking integration

**Opportunity 3: Hyperlocal Route Targeting**
- Current: Billboards = fixed location, broad reach
- Blue Ocean: "Target taxis in District 1 between 11am-2pm weekdays"
- Impact: Restaurant lunch specials, retail store openings, local events
- Timing: Phase 2, requires GPS analytics

**Opportunity 4: Real-Time Creative Swapping**
- Current: Billboard creative changes take weeks (physical installation)
- Blue Ocean: "Morning: breakfast promo, Evening: dinner promo, same day"
- Impact: QSR/retail brands optimize by daypart, weather, events
- Timing: Phase 1 capability (10-min updates), market as key differentiator

**Opportunity 5: Fleet-as-a-Service for Non-Advertisers**
- Current: Only advertising use case
- Blue Ocean: City-wide WiFi, public service announcements, emergency alerts
- Impact: Government partnerships, additional revenue streams, social impact
- Timing: Phase 3 (after core advertising proven)

---

### Bold Moves to Strengthen Blue Ocean Positioning

**Move 1: "Launch in 10 Minutes" Guarantee**
- What: Upload creative → Live on 1,000 taxis in 10 minutes or free campaign
- Why bold: Traditional DOOH takes weeks to launch
- Impact: Positions as "the fast food of DOOH" (Southwest Airlines parallel)
- Risk: Technical execution, 4G reliability
- Mitigation: MVP validation proves this is feasible

**Move 2: "No Minimum Contract" Policy**
- What: Weekly campaigns, cancel anytime, no setup fees
- Why bold: Industry standard is 3-6 month minimums
- Impact: Removes barrier for SMEs, enables seasonal/event advertisers
- Risk: Unpredictable revenue, high churn
- Mitigation: Make signup friction so low that churn doesn't matter

**Move 3: "$500 Startup Package"**
- What: 100 taxis, 1 week, 1 creative, simple web upload
- Why bold: 10x cheaper than traditional DOOH entry point
- Impact: Opens entire SME market (blue ocean non-customers)
- Risk: Margin pressure, perceived as "cheap"
- Mitigation: Volume play, upsell to larger packages over time

**Move 4: "Mobile Coverage Heatmap" Transparency**
- What: Public map showing where taxi screens are driving right now
- Why bold: Traditional DOOH hides inventory data
- Impact: Builds trust, enables self-serve targeting
- Risk: Competitors see your coverage data
- Mitigation: Data shows strength (50k vehicles), not weakness

**Move 5: "SME First" Market Entry**
- What: Launch with 20 local restaurants/shops, not Nestle/OPPO
- Why bold: Competitors always launch enterprise-first
- Impact: Proves blue ocean thesis, generates grassroots buzz, PR story
- Risk: Lower revenue per customer
- Mitigation: Build case studies to upsell enterprises later ("even SMEs use us")

## 7. KEY HYPOTHESES TO TEST (Validation Framework)

### Technical Feasibility Hypotheses (MVP - Week 1-4)

**H1: Hardware Survival**
- We believe that off-the-shelf Android tablets in weatherproof cases will survive Vietnam taxi operations with <5% failure rate over 30 days
- Test: 50 taxi pilot, track failures daily
- Success: <3 failures in 50 units over 30 days
- Pivot if: >10 failures (20%+ failure rate)

**H2: 4G Connectivity**
- We believe that 4G coverage in HCMC commercial zones is reliable enough for 80%+ device uptime
- Test: Heartbeat monitoring every 60 seconds for 30 days
- Success: 80%+ of devices report hourly
- Pivot if: <60% uptime (network insufficient)

**H3: Update Latency**
- We believe that we can push content updates to 90% of active devices within 10 minutes
- Test: Upload new creative, timestamp when each device receives it
- Success: P90 latency <10 minutes
- Pivot if: P90 latency >30 minutes (user experience too poor)

---

### Market Desirability Hypotheses (Phase 2 - Month 2-6)

**H4: SME Willingness to Pay**
- We believe that local SME brands (restaurants, retail) will pay $500/week for taxi screen advertising
- Test: Offer to 50 SMEs, track conversion rate and renewal
- Success: 10%+ take rate, 50%+ renewal after week 1
- Pivot if: <2% take rate (price too high or product not valued)

**H5: Self-Serve Adoption**
- We believe that advertisers will use web self-serve platform without sales support
- Test: Build simple upload portal, track completion rate
- Success: 70%+ complete upload without help
- Pivot if: <30% completion (too complex, needs sales support)

**H6: Performance Pricing Demand**
- We believe that performance marketers will pay CPC/CPA vs CPM-only
- Test: Offer both pricing models, track preference
- Success: 40%+ choose CPC/CPA over CPM
- Pivot if: <10% (market still thinks DOOH = awareness only)

---

### Economic Viability Hypotheses (Phase 2-3 - Month 6-12)

**H7: Unit Economics**
- We believe that revenue per taxi per month will exceed $30 with 50%+ utilization
- Test: Track revenue/taxi over 6 months at scale (1,000+ taxis)
- Success: $30+/taxi/month with $10 opex = 70%+ margin
- Pivot if: <$15/taxi/month (can't achieve profitability)

**H8: Sales Efficiency**
- We believe that self-serve CAC will be <$100 vs $2,000+ for enterprise sales
- Test: Track CAC by channel over 6 months
- Success: Self-serve CAC <$100, enterprise CAC <$1,000
- Pivot if: Self-serve CAC >$500 (acquisition too expensive)

**H9: Retention**
- We believe that monthly subscription customers will retain at 60%+ after 3 months
- Test: Cohort retention analysis
- Success: Month 3 retention >60%
- Pivot if: Month 3 retention <30% (product not sticky)

   - Run 2-week pilot campaign during MVP
   - Measure brand awareness lift or foot traffic impact
   - Generate case study for sales

3. **Document Blue Ocean Positioning**
   - Create "Mobile DOOH" category messaging (not "cheaper billboards")
   - Emphasize speed (10-min updates), flexibility (weekly campaigns), coverage (50k vehicles)
   - Prepare SME-focused marketing materials (not enterprise decks)

---
